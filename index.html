inspiration
SHADOWS ON STAIRS
Down Up is inspired by shadows on stairs. I love the zig-zag forms that are created from the shadows of rails or people on stairs, and decided to create a typeface inspired by these shadows.
process
CREATING THE LETTERFORMS
4x4 grid with blue tape
In order to map my letters, I first considered a bird’s eye view of the letters, so that they would read as normal sans-serif uppercase letters on the horizontal plane of the stairs. I created a 4x4 grid using four horizontal planes of the stairs, and placed blue tape on the vertices of this imaginary grid. I photographed these stairs from a front-facing view and side facing view.
Creating the letters in Illustrator
I uploaded my photos to Adobe Illustrator and used the blue tape as a guide to map my letters. My Regular letters are created from the front-facing view, and my oblique letters are created from the side-facing view.
Filling in the gaps
Unlike the bird’s eye view, the front-facing view and side facing views show the vertical plane of the stairs. So I used the vertical plane of the stairs as a transition between the horizontal elements.
